Dr. Alan Turing

The father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence

Here is a timeline of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912: Born in Maida Vale, London, England
  • 1927: Furthers his love of science and maths by reading Einstein whilst at Sherborne.
  • 1934: Graduates with distinction in Mathematics from King's College, Cambridge
  • 1938: Goes to Princeton University in America to study mathematics and is awarded a PhD
  • 1939: He join the Government Codes and Ciphers School and arrives at Bletchley Park the day after war is declared
  • 1940: With Gordon Welchman, Alan develops the Bombe to decipher the messages sent by the Germans using their Enigma machine.
  • 1943-45: He works with top people in the USA, which he visits to share information on code breaking.
  • 1945: At the end of the war, Alan Turing is awarded the OBE for his wartime services.
  • 1946: He publishes a paper with the first detailed design of a stored-program computer.
  • 1949: He is made deputy director of the Computing Laboratory at Manchester University.
  • 1950: He publishes the famous paper 'Computing Machinery and Intelligence' in which he develops the Turing Test.
  • 1951: He is elected Fellow of the Royal Society, and also gives talks about Artificial Intelligence on the BBC.
  • 1952: He is arrested for gross indecency. He loses his security clearance so he cannot work. He is offered chemical treatment instead of going to prison. The treatment makes him very unwell.
  • 1954: Died in Wilmslow, Cheshire
